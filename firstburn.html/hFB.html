Alexander's Confession

I believed the truth would protect me.

When accusations threatened my work and my name, I panicked. I had spent my entire life fighting to be taken seriously—to prove that I belonged in rooms where I was never meant to stand. I could not let whispers undo everything I had built.

So I wrote.

I told the world about my affair, convinced that confession was the only way to save my legacy. I told myself that honesty was courage, that exposure was necessary. I did not stop to consider who would be hurt by my words—only what would be lost if I stayed silent.

In trying to defend my reputation, I failed the person who trusted me most. I put my ambition above my marriage, my legacy above her dignity. And no explanation, no matter how carefully written, could undo that choice.

Gesite, Ongkeko
