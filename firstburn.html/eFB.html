Eliza's Response

I was never given the chance to hear it from him.

I learned the truth the same way everyone else did—through his words, printed and distributed as if my heart were a footnote. His confession did not just reveal betrayal; it made my pain public, something strangers could read, judge, and discuss.

I felt anger. I felt humiliation. But more than anything, I felt erased.

So I chose myself.

I burned his letters not out of spite, but out of survival. I refused to let my love, my forgiveness, or my suffering be used to protect his name. Some stories do not belong to history. Some truths deserve silence.

This was the moment I stepped out of his shadow—and reclaimed my own voice.
