Fifty Years More: The Enduring Legacy

The life of Alexander Hamilton may have ended in 1804, but his memory—and his story—was meticulously preserved and powerfully championed by Eliza for the five decades that followed. Explore the three pillars of her lasting impact below.

The Archive Keeper

Eliza devoted herself to meticulously collecting and organizing every letter and document of Alexander's career, ensuring his life's work survived political attacks and the passage of time.

The Children's Champion

As co-founder of the Orphan Asylum Society in New York, she established a foundational charitable institution, helping hundreds of children and defining her own life's work through tireless philanthropy.

A Story for the Ages
