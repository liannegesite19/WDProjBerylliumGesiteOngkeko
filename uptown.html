A Quiet Sorrow

The Duel

The Duel. When Philip challenged George Eacker, it was because of insults meant for me. Eacker had publicly spoken against me, and my son believed he had to respond. I even advised Philip to throw away his first shot, believing restraint would preserve honor without bloodshed. That advice haunts me.

The Retreat

The Home. After Philip’s death, Eliza wanted distance from the city, and I understood why. Uptown offered silence, but silence offered no relief. It forced me to face the truth that my ambition had placed my son in danger.

Alexander's Guilt

Guilt. I live with the belief that Philip died because he wanted to defend my honor. I taught him to value reputation and courage, and those lessons led him to that duel. That guilt is something I will never escape.

Eliza's Quiet Forgiveness

Forgiveness. Eliza’s forgiveness is a mercy I did not earn. Despite my failures as a husband and father, she chose compassion. Her forgiveness does not erase my guilt, but it is the only thing that keeps me standing.

Gesite, Ongkeko
